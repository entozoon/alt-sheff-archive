Global Justice meeting

Thu 7 Jan 2015, 7pm, Quaker Meeting House, 10 St James St. S1 2EW

Global Justice (formerly World Development Movement) aims to change the actions, policies and institutions of governments, corporations and wealthy minorities, which are preventing an end to poverty and injustice. Part of a global movement, it works alongside people protesting in the developing world.

Meetings are held on the first Thursday of each month, to discuss coming events and campaigns. Usually followed by informal gathering in a nearby pub.

Current Affairs class

Thu 7 Jan 2016, 10.30am, Victoria Hall Methodist Church, Norfolk St. S1 2JB

A discussion course looking at social, environmental, political and ethical issues, UK and world-wide. The detailed topics will be agreed within the class. The course runs on Thursdays 10.30am - 12.30pm, for 11 weeks starting 7th January 2016. Last meeting March 17th, with no break at half-term.


Courses need a minimum number of 12 participants in order to run and have an upper limit of 16 for this course. So if you cannot get to the first week, please contact tutor (Paul Boizot) first to say that you intend to come later, and if you only find out about a course after week 1, contact tutor to find out if there is a place for you, in case the course is either full or cancelled. The best way to guarantee a place is to enrol in advance online or by phone. Online and phone enrolments are limited to 14, leaving just 2 places for people to enrol "on the day" so advance booking is recommended.
