Introduction to Woodland Coppicing

Sat 15 Feb 2014, 9.30-4pm, J G Graves Woodland Discovery Centre, Ecclesall Woods, Abbey Lane S7 2QZ

A one-day course about native woodlands and their management, covering basic theory, hands-on practical experience and tree identification. Aspects covered on the course include planning the work area, looking at various devices, building work breaks and coppice restoration, working methods, practical coppicing, material selection, processing, and storage. This course will show you how to take neglected woodland and transform it into a productive coppice with wildlife benefits.

The venue is ancient woodland near the JG Graves Woodland Discovery Centre, off Abbey lane, Sheffield. Price Â£80.

Free Cakes For Kids launch

Sat 15 Feb 2014, 11:30am-3pm, The Carpenter Room, Sheffield Central Library, Surrey St S1 1XZ

Fundraising cake and cookbook sale.

Free Cakes For Kids bake birthday cakes for families who find it hard to provide a cake for their child. The cakes are baked by local volunteers with the child's favourite theme in mind. It is free, friendly and confidential. Families can call themselves, or are referred by organisations who work with families in Sheffield.

Cupids In Nooses: Anti-Valentines Festval

Fri 14-Sun 16 Feb 2014, The Rude Shipyard, 89 Abbeydale Rd. S7 1FE

It's back! Let the love-sick and loved-up join together to poke a big stick of fun at the big commercial beast, with another weekend of music, poetry, comedy, competitions, real ales from Blue Bee Brewery, great cake and anti-valentines silliness as the Rude Shipyard presents its Anti-Valentines Festival. Free entry, with a donations box to be split between all performers.

Friday 14th (normal opening in daytime.) From 6pm an evening of live music with Nick Maldini of Screaming Maldini, virtuoso folk guitarist Jim Ghedi playing with viola player Llewelyn, soulful singer song writer James Tait with his new band - Pomegranate the Fruit, Magpies and Woolly Mammoth.

Saturday 15th a whole day of events; midday open mic poetry from a Shipping Forecast anti-valentines special, stand-up from Stan Skinny, the Cupids in Nooses anti-valentines poetry competition from Wordlife, (entries for the must be submitted to cupids@therudeshipyard.com by Friday 7th February and the winner will be published in Now Then Magazine). Then a fantastic bill of live music from Red Trees, Leon O'Doherty, Jupiter in Jars, Over the Yardarm, Ruth Theodore, Captives on the Carousel, Five Leaves Nettles, Every Animal on Earth and The Purgatory Players.

Sunday 16th from 5pm an evening of independent films from local film makers. 'Taking the Michael' by Ian Nesbitt, 'The Bounds of Habitation' by Rosie Etherington and two super shorts by Thomas Payton-Greene and Danielle Swindells.

www.therudeshipyard.com
