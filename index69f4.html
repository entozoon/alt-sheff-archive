Chile: Arpilleras, Photographic and Political Caricatures

From 7pm on Mon 4 to Fri 8 Nov 2013, Exhibition Space, New Jessop Building, University of Sheffield

40 years ago a military coup plunged Chile into a brutal right-wing dictatorship, the first country in the world to experience the shock of "neoliberalism". Many fled the country, some arriving here to make Sheffield their new home.

Now, in commemoration of the anniversary, there is a series of events including this exhibition "Arpilleras, Photographic and Political Caricatures". Launched Monday 4th at 7pm, the exhibition will be on display until 8th November.

Sheffield Equality Group monthly meeting

Wed 6 Nov 2013, 7pm Quaker Meeting House, 10 St James St. S1 2EW

Sheffield Equality Group meetings is an open space where people can discuss and plan actions around income equality, an
autonomous group affiliated to the national Equality Trust. All welcome. This time, launch of the Basic Income survey, with practice and discussion for those interested in interviewing friends, family, associates and the anonymous! Contributing to a wider discussion about welfare and benefits in Sheffield.

Workshop/Consultation: "Refreshing the Sheffield Food Strategy"

Wed 6 Nov, morning, at City Hall.

There have been many changes in Sheffield since Sheffield produced its first Food Plan in 2011. Some changes have been positive, for example the continuing success of the Food Festival, action to improve School Food and the soon to be opened new Market on the Moor. Unfortunately there have also been cuts in welfare and the appearance of Food Banks in the city. The time is right for a refresh of the Food Plan - (now draft Food Strategy) for Sheffield.

Please contact Jill Lancaster Jill.Lancaster@sheffield.gcsx.gov.uk tel Sheff 2057469 for further information about this event or if you wish to be part of a virtual network of people who have an interest in this area.
