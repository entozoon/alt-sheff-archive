National March for Dignity

Sat 29 June 2013

Sheffield joins in the National March for Dignity as part of Refugee Week, representing a call for dignity for asylum seekers and an end to destitution.

11am Meet at Ellesmere Green (Burngreave, at top of Spital Hill) to march through Sheffield

12.30pm Arrival at City Hall for a gathering with speakers.

1.00pm Leaving via The Moor

Destination St Mary's, Bramall Lane, where refreshments will be available.

This march is organised by refugee and asylum community groups in Sheffield, who encourage everyone to join in to show solidarity. Refugee Week is a UK-wide programme of arts, cultural and educational events that celebrates the contribution of refugees to the UK and promotes better understanding of why people seek sanctuary. Anyone can take part by organising, attending or taking part in activities.

2013 Stop HS2 Convention

Sat 29 Jun 2013 (Staffordshire)

Are you against the High Speed Rail 2 which could come through Sheffield? Do you want to see it stopped? Do you want to learn more about the project and how to campaign against it? Then come to the second Stop HS2 National Convention.

Thirty sessions on all aspects of the project and what you can do. You will have the chance to meet with people up and down the line, including those who have been living under the shadow of HS2 for the last three years.

Held at the Staffordshire County Showground it will be a crash course on all aspects of the HS2 project and campaigning against it.

Contributors include Deputy Leader of the Green Party, Will Duckworth, all of the main groups opposing HS2: Stop HS2, HS2 Action Alliance, AGHAST and 51m and both the Woodland Trust and the Wildlife Trusts explaining the environmental devastation which HS2 would bring. Economics professors will explain the holes in the business and economics case.

Full details at http://stophs2.org/convention
