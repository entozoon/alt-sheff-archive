Family Allotment Playschemes

Thu 21 Aug 2014, 1pm-3pm, Firth Park Community Allotment

Summer holidays are here! The regular toddler groups at Firth Park Community Allotment are making way for family playschemes for the school holidays, Thursdays 1pm-3pm on 31st July, 7th, 14th and 21st August. Activities on offer include bug hunts, flower dyes, face painting, fruit smoothies, potato day, plus the usual activities - sand tray, mud kitchen, dino park, crafts, etc.

If you haven't visited before, the allotment site is specially designed for those with young families and is much more than a growing plot. And it has just been awarded its Green Flag status again! Firth Park Community Allotment is part of Heeley City Farm's family of growing sites. The allotment is at the meeting point of Vickers Road and Drive - just look for the big green allotment gates and follow the signs. Ring for more info: Dot 07985 421346.

Summer Craft Sessions

Thu 21 Aug 2014, 12-3pm, Heeley City Farm (Energy Centre) 25 Alexandra Rd. S2 3EE

Heeley City Farm is running extra crafts sessions in the Energy Centre every Thursday during the school holidays, on 24th and 31st July, 7th, 14th, 21st and 28th August, 12-3pm. Glitter, glue and sunshine! Wear clothes you don't mind getting messy in! The Farm doesn't receive any funding for these activities, so cost will help to pay for materials and raise some money for the Farm to keep going too. All children must be supervised by their parents/carers whilst doing crafts. Sheffield Children's University Credits can be collected here.

Ring Sharon for more information on Sheff 3039981 or email sharon.syec@heeleyfarm.org.uk. Heeley City Farm is 20 mins walk from Sheffield, or catch the No 47 bus to Herdings from town - it drops you right outside the Farm. Â£1 contribution for each child taking part, grown-ups free.

"Cut off" - an exhibition of photos

All of Aug 2014, Weds-Sat 11am-4pm, Bank St. Arts

"Cut off" is an exhibition of photos taken by people who have been refused asylum, exploring their circumstances, experiences, hopes and frustrations.

It forms part of a general exhibition on the theme "Cut", at Bank Street Arts, 32-40 Bank Street, Sheffield, S1 2DS.
