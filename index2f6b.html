Global Justice Sheffield meeting

Thu 15 Jan 2015, 7pm Dog and Partridge pub-side room, 56 Trippet Lane S1 4EL

The organisation formerly known as World Development Movement has, from January, changed its name to Global Justice Now. For this meeting, there is a change from the usual 1st Thursday of the month to the 3rd, and there is also a change of venue.

Global Justice Now aims to change the actions, policies and institutions of governments, corporations and wealthy minorities, which are preventing an end to poverty and injustice. Part of a global movement, it works alongside people protesting in the developing world, on issues like Ghana being pressured to pass a new law that will restrict small-scale farmers and food producers from freely saving, exchanging and selling seeds. This will side-line traditional varieties and push farmers towards buying seeds from big business like Monsanto year after year. And small-scale farmers could even face criminalisation for saving seeds that corporations lay claim to.

Public Lecture: The Rise of Innovation Districts

Thu 15 Jan 2015, 7.30pm-8.30pm (doors open 7pm) Sheffield Cathedral, Church St. S1 1HA

Bruce Katz, internationally renowned policy adviser on economic and urban development, comes to Sheffield for a public lecture on the innovation district model. Bruce regularly advises public, private and civic leaders on sustainable policy reform; he co-led the housing and urban transition team for the Obama Administration and was Chief of Staff to the Secretary of Housing and Urban Development under President Clinton.

Bruce will be joined by Julie Wagner, Senior Fellow at the Metropolitan Policy Program, and Kelly Kline, Economic Development Director of the City of Fremont, California for a panel discussion and Questions & Answers.

Organised by the University of Sheffield, Sheffield City Council and Creative Sheffield. This is a free, ticketed event. Booking here: http://goo.gl/BlNxKU
