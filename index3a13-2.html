Oxjam 2013

October, various venues

Oxjam Music Festival takes place every October, with hundreds of volunteer-run events all around the UK, to raise money to help Oxfam fight poverty and suffering around the world.

Oxjam began in 2006, with the aim of creating a network of music-loving people across the UK. You could organise your own event, or perform or help out at a local Oxjam gig.

Sheffield gigs will be listed here separately. UK-wide details at www.oxfam.org.uk/oxjam

Carbon Capital speaker tour - Sheffield

Wed 30 Oct 2013, 7pm, Venue: Quaker Meeting House, 10 St. James St. S1 2EW

Banks, hedge funds and pension funds based in Britain are bankrolling the extraction of fossil fuels all over the world. Many of these dirty energy projects are wrecking communities and fuelling climate change while doing nothing to increase energy access for local people. Join WDM (World Development Movement) to demand energy justice. See more at: www.wdm.org.uk/events/carbon-capital-speaker-tour

From 20th to 31st October, WDM local groups across the UK will be hosting guests from Colombia and Indonesia who are involved in opposing the expansion of open-cast coal mining in their countries. WDM Sheffield will be hosting their meeting on Wednesday 30th October, and the speakers will be:

Hendrik Sirengar, the co-ordinator of JATAM, the Indonesian mining advocacy network which works with communities affected by mining.

Kirsty Wright from WDM, explaining the role of the Carbon Capital campaign in reigning in this dirty energy production.

This event is free and open to all.

Public meeting: "Racism, Resistance and Revolution"

Wed 30 Oct, 7pm, Central United Reformed Church, 60 Norfolk St. S1 2JB

A Socialist Workers Party public meeting with Weyman Bennett, contributor to new book Say it Loud! Marxism and the Fight Against Racism. Racism, from rebellions against slavery in the 18th century to the fight against police harassment and the racism of UKIP, EDL and the Tories is a radical history of black and white working class unity. This public meeting will counter the myth that racism is inevitable. It is an oppressive part of capitalism, and it will put the case for revolutionary struggle to end both.
