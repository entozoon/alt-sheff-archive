Festival of Debate: "Fairness on the 83"

Throughout April, Showroom Cinema, 15 Paternoster Row S1 2BX

In 2013 an independent report found that life expectancy changes by up to ten years across Sheffield‚Äôs 83 bus route, which links links Millhouses in the south with Ecclesfield in the north. "Fairness on the 83" is a film and photography project mapping the city and talking to members of the public about fairness and inequality in Sheffield. A selection of photos from the project will be on display at the Showroom throughout April and video interviews are online at nowthenmagazine.com/fairnessonthe83.

The Festival of Debate is taking place in Sheffield in the run-up to the General election, during March and April. It provides opportunities to get together and discuss a range of issues which affect everyone. Informed decision making and strategic planning are essential for democracy, so come and get involved. They also welcome volunteers, from attending events and taking part in discussions to volunteering time to help run an event, promote the festival and much more.

General Election Hustings: Hallam Constituency

Wed 29 Apr 2015, 7.30pm Wesley Hall, Crookes S10 1UD

"Crookes Forum" and "Sheffield for Democracy" are jointly hosting this event, to give voters in the Hallam Constituency the opportunity to question candidates in person.

Present will be:
Peter Garbutt, Green
Oliver Coppard, Labour

Talk: "Positive Money"

Wed 29 Apr 2015, 7.30pm, Quaker Meeting House, 10 St James St. S1 2EW

An illustrated talk by Peter Verity. (This is a repeat of a talk given as part of the Festival of Debate on 22nd April, which was sold out).

Few people ask the question ‚ÄúWhere does money come from?‚Äù. The surprising answer is that 97% of money under our current capitalist system is just created by banks when they make loans. This talk will illustrate how the nation has surrendered its sovereign right to create money to private banks, and how this leads to high levels of private debt, and a transfer of money from the poor to the rich.

Positive Money is a campaign to create a money and banking system that works for society and not against it. Peter Verity is the coordinator of the Sheffield group of Positive Money supporters.
