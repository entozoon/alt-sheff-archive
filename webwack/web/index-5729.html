Five charged after trees protest

Nov/December 2016

Share on

  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Digg

December update:

It has been revealed that a police Silver Command was set up to deal with tactics during the controversial early morning tree-cutting operation in November 2016. This is an operational unit to ensure co-ordinated Police actions, following multi-agency planning meetings usually involving civic leaders and other authorities. Residents of Rustlings Road were woken in the early hours of the morning and ordered to remove their cars. Protesters gathered and eventually five were arrested, before the work went ahead.

The tree removal is part of the City Council Private Finance Initiative (PFI) contract with the massive AMEY corporation, for maintenance of city roads over 25 years.

All five arrested (including two women in their 70s) were later charged by the Police, with offences relating to preventing people from working. This was under legislation passed by the Conservative government, designed to suppress trade union activity. The first of the protesters appeared at Sheffield Magistrates Court on 1st December, accompanied by a protest rally of city-wide opponents of the tree removal plan. They were ordered to return for trial on 9th March 2017. The charges could have been thrown out by the Magistrates at this stage, but the Crown Prosecution Service will now be bringing evidence.

Dave Dillner and Helen McIlroy from Sheffield Tree Action Groups (STAG) were interviewed about the issue on Sheffield Live Radio
