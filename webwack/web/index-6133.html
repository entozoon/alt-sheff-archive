Freecycle or Freegle?

Sept 2009 (Updated 2015)

Share on

  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Digg

There have been developments with Freecycle, the international and UK charity aiming to keep good stuff out of landfills. Rotherham Freecycle and the Sheffield North-and-West group have moved over to a new organisation - "Freegle", still dedicated to the same aims.

The main reason for the move is that although Freecycle is a great idea, UK groups were forced to work under a USA-based system. UK leaders of Freecycle tried to get freedom to run things here in a way to suit UK law, but without success.

In July 2009, 4 leading members of the UK Freecycle team resigned. Moderators around the country then formed an Independent Association of Freecycle Moderators and again tried talks with The Freecycle Network. No success. Instead an increasing number of group owners and moderators have been summarily dismissed from Freecycle including the former director of Freecycle UK. Since then things have settled down, there are Freegle and Freecycle groups around the UK. The groups are still grassroots and moderated by local people.

So... what are the local groups?
