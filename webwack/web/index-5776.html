Greens hit the roof as Council climbs down

January 2016

Share on

  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Digg

Sheffield Green Party are furious at the Labour administration for not going through with plans to fit solar panels on the roofs of 6000 homes. As well as the environment aspect, they point out this is bad news for tenants struggling with fuel poverty; the solar panels would have saved money.

The Greens are angry because of long delays to the project which was first proposed in the Green Budget Amendment for 2014/15. It was rejected at the time, only to resurface the following year as a Labour proposal. The decision to cancel in January 2016 was blamed on Government cuts in subsidies, but Greens argue that cuts are nothing new.

The delay followed by cancellation means that thousands of council tenants won't receive promised benefits, millions of pounds previously available in subsidies have been lost, and carbon emissions won't now be cut. Rob Cole, Green Party candidate, said “This is the second time Labour has ended a council solar power project and this time the delays have cost Sheffield dear. If they had been prepared to work with the Greens for the good of the city thousands of residents would now be benefiting from free and clean electricity ... We need a Council that is prepared to listen to different views and work together with all groups to get the best for Sheffield.”

Sheffield Green Party
