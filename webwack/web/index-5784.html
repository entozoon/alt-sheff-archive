Petition against council cuts

January 2016

Share on

  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Digg

The government intends to remove all central government funding for local councils by 2020, who will then have to make up the gap from business rates, or selling off council property such as parks. This will be disastrous for poorer cities such as Sheffield, which has already suffered Â£300 million cuts since 2010.

While local councils are not to blame for this, are they doing enough to fight against it, or just acting as managers for the endless cuts?

Delegates at the National Conference of the People's Assembly in London on Dec 5th passed motions calling for a campaign against further cuts by local councils, and a petition has now been set up to promote a debate at the full council meeting on Feb 3rd.
