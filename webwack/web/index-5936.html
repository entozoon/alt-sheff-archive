Sustainable energy community shares at full power

Community Share Offer open until 31st December 2012

Share on

  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Digg

Electricity will be generated using a fish-friendly Archimedes screw hydro-generator, on the River Don at Blackburn Meadows between Sheffield and Rotherham. The plant will produce energy for 80 homes and save Sheffield 170 tonnes of CO2 per year. Construction should begin in 2013. Sheffield Renewables is developing this green hydro-power project, with supporters investing £250 per share, and the rest through commercial financing. It will be a first for Sheffield, and England's largest community-owned hydro scheme. Profits will be used to fund local green initiatives, including more renewable energy.

Sheffield Renewables is a community and a social enterprise run by and for local people, and a registered Industrial & Provident Society co-operative. Much of its work done by enthusiastic volunteers. It is not-for-profit, so surplus money generated is re-invested to support local sustainability. The work helps to strengthen social cohesion and community resilience by encouraging people to work together and to share a common purpose, as well as by developing community-owned assets. They operate across the city for the benefit of everyone, with an additional focus on areas close to project sites.

Community Shares are a way for people to invest in projects and to be involved in change to a greener way of life. This follows other local organisations Portland Works and Hazelhurst Community Supported Agriculture successfully raising funds for community schemes. As well as being an attractive ethical investment opportunity, investors receive a return of up to 3% interest, and wider social and environmental benefits. The minimum stake is £250 and the maximum is £20,000. The Community Share Offer is open until December 31st 2012. To find out more information about Sheffield Renewables and how to invest, please email emma@sheffieldrenewables.org.uk
