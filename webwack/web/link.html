Recently Added

ACORN

ACORN is a community-based union of working class people. They run large scale member-led campaigns supporting and empowering low-income communities across the country to fight for a better life, on issues like bad rented housing, and public transport. Sheffield ACORN email: sheffield@acorncommunities.org.uk, tel: 07508 958011, Facebook page.

Sheffield Fruit Trees

Originally a project of Grow Sheffield, this group promotes tree planting in gardens, allotments, waste spaces, etc. They offer training in grafting and pruning, and preserve local tree varieties raised without chemicals and suited to a Yorkshire climate.

Sheffield Marxist Society

The University of Sheffield has a Marxist Society, one of 30 around the UK. They usually meet on Thursdays 6pm-8pm for discussion, and also get involved with activism on issues ranging from climate change to international solidarity.

Unity Edible Gardens

A Sheffield-based company specialising in edible and wildlife-friendly gardens, started by James Adams. Whether you have a small balcony or a large community space, they can help you create a beautiful, productive space beneficial to people and wildlife, based on organic and permaculture principles.

SheFest

SheFest champions women’s rights and gender equality for all self-defining women, through inclusive events, activities, supportive networks and empowering opportunities. A not-for-profit organisation made up of local volunteers. They run an annual fringe festival each March, around International Women’s Day.

LGBT+ Hub

A forum for lesbian, gay, bisexual, trans and people of other minority sexual orientations and/or gender identities under the LGBT+ umbrella, and their allies. It raises issues specific to LGBT+ communities, promoting fairness and inclusion and challenging inequalities. Also collaborations marking awareness days including LGBT History Month (Feb), International Day Against Homophobia, Transphobia and Biphobia (17 May), Transgender Day of Remembrance (20 Nov), and World AIDS Day (1 Dec).
