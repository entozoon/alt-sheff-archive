Fair Deal for Sheffield?

Jan/Feb 2013

Share on

  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Digg

A broad-based campaign has been launched, opposing the brutal "austerity" attacks on local government spending, amounting to a cut of 1/3 for our city, while more affluent parts of the country are being let off more lightly. This is clearly unfair and Sheffield and South Yorkshire do not deserve this, so local figures from David Blunkett MP to the Bishop of Sheffield are supporting this campaign. It includes a petition against the cuts. 5000 signatures would initiate a special council meeting to bring pressure on national government to undo the unfairness.

(Also note Sheffield Anti-Cuts Alliance (SACA) is currently debating and protesting the issue - including at their 12th February meeting (see events page) - is it a question of which area deserves more or less cuts? Or is it deeper than that?
