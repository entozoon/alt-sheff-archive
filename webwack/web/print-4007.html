Conference: Healthy Air in Sheffield

Date, Time & Location Fri 2 Dec 2011, 9 for 9.30am to 12.30, Quaker Meeting House, 10 St James St. S1 2EW
Details The Healthy Air Campaign in partnership with Sheffield City Council, NHS Sheffield and East End Quality of Life Initiative invite you to the Healthy Air In Sheffield conference. The campaign believes that if we were able to clean up our air in the 1950s, we can make our air healthy today. With political will from the top and action from the ground up, this public health crisis can be stopped in its tracks. A significant proportion of air pollution is caused by vehicle emissions, particularly the increasing number of diesel vehicles. Active transport choices can improve health, including obesity and heart disease. The Healthy Air Campaign seeks to increase public awareness and understanding of the health impacts of air pollution and help communities mitigate those impacts and adapt to reduce them. It will achieve this by promoting active lifestyles as part of a joined-up sustainable transport, air quality and public health approach within communities, action to effectively monitor, report and reduce air pollution, and advocating a new government air quality and public health strategy.
