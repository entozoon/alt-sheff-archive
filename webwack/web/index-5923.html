Chapeltown: Coal mining may resume

Spring 2013

Share on

  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Digg

"Tears and anger and disgust" were expressed as planning permission was granted to dig up a coal tip near Chapeltown. In January 2013 a Sheffield Council planning committee agreed that "Recycoal" can destroy an oak woodland, dig up a hill, and burn hundreds of thousands of tonnes of coal.

Despite strong fears about the health impacts of such a project, the likelihood that this will diminish the local environment for people and wildlife, and the sheer folly of adding an extra million tonnes of CO2 to the atmosphere, this was declared "environmentally acceptable" and the impacts on health said to be not worth assessing. A hundred community members watched as the councillors and officers played out this tragedy on the public stage, powerless to change the ending. A local group Cowley Residents Action Group (CRAG) are now looking at whether judicial review of this decision is possible.
