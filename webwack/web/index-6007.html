Justice for Djoly: an update

December 2010

Share on

  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Digg

Bavwidi Mpanzu (or 'Djoly' to his many friends), seeking asylum from repression in the Democratic Republic of Congo, was jailed for working. But even though his sentence is over he remains locked up and is under threat of deportation: one 'crime', two punishments. Djoly has received huge support: nearly 1000 signatures; frequent prison visits; dozens of personal letters of support; backed by Sheffield Trades Council and even David Blunkett MP. A demonstration for him in November attracted 30-40 people outside Sheffield Town Hall.

What You Can Do: The Justice for Djoly Campaign is trying to raise £1000 for his bail application (got about £600 already). You can donate to the campaign by making cheques payable to 'SYMAAG', marking the back of your cheque 'Djoly' and posting it to SYMAAG, Scotia Works, Leadmill Road, Sheffield S1 4SE

David Blunkett MP has said that he supports Djoly, contact and ask him to stop Djoly being doubly punished by being deported. Write to: 2nd Floor, MidCity House, 17-21 Furnival Gate, Sheffield S1 4QR or email blunkettd@parliament.uk
