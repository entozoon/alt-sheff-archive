Co-operative Bank - ethical no more?

Update January 2016

Share on

  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Digg

In a shocking move, the Co-operative Bank uddenly closed down the accounts of 20 organisations supporting Palestine, with no explanation other referring to its ”risk appetite”. The list includes three local organisations; Sheffield Palestine Solidarity Campaign (SPSC), Sheffield Palestine Women’s Scholarship Fund, and Yorkshire Palestine Cultural Exchange.

The Palestine Solidarity Campaign is taking legal action against the bank, accusing it of discrimination and contravening the Equality Act 2010. Its Director, Sarah Colborne, said ”It is quite clear that the Co-operative Bank no longer cares about human rights – the Palestinians suffer incursions on their human rights day in, day out at the hands of an occupying force that continues to violate international law. In the UK we have a great tradition of solidarity organisations coming together to stand against human rights violations throughout the world – this is something that the Co-operative Bank should support and not punish.”

There is no evidence (yet) of Israeli influence on the decision, and the bank denies this, but there was a fear that the ethical policies of the Co-operative Bank would be compromised, after the near-collapse resulted in a buy-out by US hedge funds, of around 80% of its equity. No regulations stipulate Palestine to be a high risk destination for funds, so the decision to classify Palestine as high risk is the Co-op Bank’s decision alone.

UPDATE 1: The move follows closure of the Cuba Solidarity Campaign account. Activists are warning that a wider pattern is emerging, of stifling activity by removal of facilities, based on a suggestion of guilt by association, with neither any proof nor right of appeal.

UPDATE 2: Palestine support groups open accounts in Yorkshire. Sheffield Palestine Solidarity Campaign now has an account with Yorkshire Bank. Sheffield Palestine Women's Scholarship Fund has one with Yorkshire Building Society. Please contact them for details if you wish to become a supporter of these respectable, hard-working and long-established organisations.
