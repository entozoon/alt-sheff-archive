A Personal Geography of Injustice: A talk by Daniel Dorling

Date, Time & Location Sun 17 Oct 2010, 7pm at Showroom Cinema, Paternoster Row, Sheffield S1 2BX
Details Daniel Dorling, Professor of Human Geography at the University of Sheffield, is a leading member of the team that has developed the website Worldmapper, and author of Injustice: Why Social Inequality Persists. This talk, using film, music, graphs, cartoons and maps, covers five aspects of injustice: elitism, exclusion, prejudice, greed and despair. Geography lecturer Carl Lee, from Sheffield College, will question him about his thinking and how all this impacts upon the lives of people in Sheffield and beyond.
