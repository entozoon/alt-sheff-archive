North Sheffield needs Volunteer Rangers

January 2013

Share on

  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Digg

Trans Pennine Trail, the national coast to coast route for recreation and transport, by walkers, cyclists and (in parts) horse riders, needs help in North Sheffield. Three Volunteer Rangers are wanted, to cover Chapeltown to Parsons Cross, Parsons Cross to Meadowhall, and a Signing Champion for North Sheffield.

With 350 miles of the Trans Pennine Trail to look after, Volunteer Rangers fill a vital role and act as eyes and ears on the ground on a regular basis, dealing with minor problems and reporting major ones. A perfect excuse to get out into the countryside (if you need one). Includes things like reporting missing signs and cutting back overhanging branches, litter picking, reporting potholes and other hazards, organising work days, helping to fundraise, guided walks and rides. The scheme operates in association with Sustrans, the sustainable transport charity.

To get involved or for more information contact Tim Hess, Volunteer Group Co-ordinator: hesspedmail@googlemail.com tel. 07795 058968 or Mandy Loach, Trans Pennine Trail Officer, Barnsley Metropolitan Borough Council, tel. 01226 772005
