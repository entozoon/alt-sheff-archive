Council launches Climate Change Fund

October 2011

Share on

  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Digg

The Council's Community Assembly Climate Change Fund round for 2011/12 has just been launched on 21st October, with a closing date of Friday 25 November. Successful projects will be able to access their money from January.

There is a total of £280,000 of capital funding available, with £30,000 available to be spent on projects in each Community Assembly, and £70,000 earmarked to be spent on Eco Schools (ES) projects across the 7 Assembly areas. Funding must be spent within the financial year the funding is awarded (31 March 2012).

The Eco Schools element (£10k in each Assembly area) is being handled by a separate, parallel process. Contact the Eco Schools team for more details (Steve Byers) - Email: steve.byers@sheffield.gov.uk Tel Sheff 2734641.

The Fund is available to not-for-profit community organisations, social enterprises, and public sector organisations working in partnership with communities and/or not-for-profit organisations (private businesses and individual households and groups of houses are not eligible).

The Fund is for energy and non-energy projects which will reduce carbon emissions AND raise awareness of climate change in your local community. Examples include:
* Energy efficiency measures including insulation, upgrading of heating systems, and installation of low energy lighting in community buildings
* Installation of renewable / low carbon energy generation equipment
* Provision of equipment for community cycling schemes, equipment to support the operation of car clubs, measures to help food growing

Application forms and Guidance documentation, with full eligibility details, are available from your local Community Assembly, or by personal request. The general enquiries number for the Assemblies is Sheff 2736849.
