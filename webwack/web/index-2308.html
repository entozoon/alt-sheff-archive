How can elderly people stay in their own home? What are the options?

Mon 14 Jul 2014, 3.15 for 3.30pm-6pm, 1 St Paul’s Place, S1 2JX

Share on
  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Digg

DLA Piper host events like this for people to have fun and help 'decision-makers', and to be better informed. This one is about 'key issues' in elderly people's lives: financial austerity; ageing populations; housing crisis; high cost of falls in the over 60s; "asset rich, income poor"; fuel poverty and deep poverty; sale of family assets for basic survival. Speakers:Rosalind Eve, Chief Executive, Age UK SheffieldJohn Grant, Senior Lecturer, Sheffield Hallam University Teresa Hitchcock, Partner, DLA Piper LLPEddie Murphy, Technical Director, Mott MacDonaldJacques Pender, Head of Automation & Robotics, Sheffield Hallam UniversityFacilitators: Perry Walker & Wendy SternBased on Crowd Wise, developed by Perry Walker while at New Economics Foundation (nef), designed to help people reach consensus on tricky issues. It asks a simple question, uses four to six 'policy options' with expert briefings and small group work. You advocate your option and cast your vote with preference voting. Some people will work in groups on their preference while others have to 'play a role'. Preference voting encourages people who disagree to speak constructively by explaining their group's option to the other groups so that consensus can be reached by group mergers or amending options to be more 'appealing'.The venue is opposite the Peace Gardens between Cafe Nero and Cafe Rouge. Booking free but note £15 Standard Charge for anyone who books a place without giving 48 hours' notice if cancelled.Action for Involvement creates space for people to get together with key policy makers.
