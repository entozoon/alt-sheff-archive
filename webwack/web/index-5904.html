New resources for co-operatives

Open now (official launch 19th Feb 2014)

Share on

  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Digg

Co-operatives are on the rise, and now a Yorkshire Co-operative Resource Centre has been opened at Aizlewood's Mill, Nursery Street in Sheffield. Established by Sheffield Co-operative Development Group and Co-operatives Yorkshire & Humber, it offers resources, learning, skill-sharing and information, open to anyone for specific research or to get wider understanding of the co-operative movement.

Reading and education rooms were a common feature of co-op stores in the early years but things changed, and co-operative education became more centralised, formal and distant. But now things are changing.

Education is Co-operative Principle number 5, so the new centre is to be called "Principle Five". It offers a lending library, archives of news, journals, reports, materials on The Co-operative College, Fairtrade, The Co-operative Party, Co-operatives UK and other co-operative organisations, and even a graphic novel about The Co-operative Revolution!
