Transition Sheffield Skills for Sustainability workshops

Sat 1 & Sun 2 Oct 2011

Share on
  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Digg

Transition Sheffield is hosting four workshops, the first in a series of skills programmes. Details for each are below. Planting soft fruit, growing your own garlic, a simple introduction Saturday 1st October 10am-12.30pm Venueto be confirmed. Learn the basics of how to plant and grow blackcurrants, redcurrants, gooseberries. How to propagatethem by hardwood cuttings. Raspberry basics. The basics of growing garlic. Information sheets to summarise important learning points. Hopefully a small amount of practical hands-on stuff. 8-10 places. Contact details: Jerry Simon 07976 313642 jerry.simon@hotmail.co.ukLow Impact Living: From technical fixes to lifestyle changes Saturday 1st October 2pm-5pm Fireside Housing Co-op, Melrose Road, Burngreave. Terry and Rick have both been involved in projects aimed at reducing the environmental cost of meeting basic needs for 25 years. They are both members of the Fireside Housing Co-op in Sheffield. This workshop offers the chance to visit Fireside. It will include an illustrated talk on low impact living followed by questions and dicussion. 20 places. Contact: Rick or Terry on 0114 2725908 rickwatson50@hotmail.comChutney Making Sunday 2nd October 1.30-4pm Crookes. This workshop is held in collaboration with Sheffield Abundance who will provide the apples. It includes preparation of fruit and vegetables, cooking with additional ingredients, sterilisation of jars and sealing for preservation. 6 places. Contact Ruth Davenport rudek@tiscali.co.ukCreating A Low Energy House Sunday 2nd October 2-5pm. 21 Eastgrove Rd, S10 2NN This workshop is based at a house which is a member of the Old Home Super Home network in Sheffield 10 (www.superhomes.org.uk). Learn facts and figures on the costs and performance of various energy saving measures including photovoltaics (solar electricity), solar water heating, condensation gas boiler, wood-burning stove, passive solar heating from a conservatory, insulation measures etc. 20 places. Each workshop costs £2 for unwaged/£3 for waged.If you would like to attend a workshop please contact the relevant person. Please book quickly as places are limited. General information: Amyan MacFadyen tel Sheff 2681148 or amyan@gn.apc.org
