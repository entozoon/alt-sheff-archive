Sheffield Fairness Commission awaits your views

Deadline 4th April 2012

Share on

  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Digg

After pressure from Sheffield Equality Group and others, a Fairness Commission has been established by Sheffield City Council. It will assess inequalities in the city and to make equalisation recommendations, just like a Parliamentary Select Committee. The inquiry is now taking evidence and will produce a report by September 2012.

Anyone, or any Sheffield group, wishing to submit evidence is invited to do it by 4th April. Maximum 2500 words, ideally with a bullet point summary. The following questions will be addressed: What specific evidence do you hold about inequalities and fairness that may be of use to the Commission? What is your analysis of the cause/s of inequalities within Sheffield? Any examples of good practice in relation to reducing inequalities and increasing fairness that the Commission should be aware of? (In the city, elsewhere in the UK, or overseas) What do you believe would be the best way to tackle inequalities and increase fairness in the city? What should be the top 3 priorities for the city?

The Commission is particularly interested in health inequalities, poverty, welfare and benefits, employment, unemployment, aspiration, attainment and skills, housing and environmental issues, voice, participation and inclusion, access to services and transport. All written evidence submitted will be published, but not the personal details of individuals.

To submit evidence or for more information email: fairness.commission@sheffield.gov.uk, or by post: Fairness Commission, Policy, Partnerships & Research, Sheffield City Council, Town Hall, Sheffield, S1 2HH
