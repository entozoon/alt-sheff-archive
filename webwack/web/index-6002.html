Campaigning against the Azure Card

December 2010

Share on

  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Digg

The Azure card was introduced a year ago to replace the use of supermarket vouchers for asylum seekers whose claim has been refused but who are still unable to return to their country. The card is topped up weekly with £35 for a single person and can be used in a limited number of supermarket outlets. It cannot be exchanged for cash, and only £5 can be carried over to the next week.

Refugee Council recently issued a damning report on the card Your inflexible friend: the cost of living without cash and in response to a Freedom of Information request, Northern Refugee Centre reported that a staggering £10 was being lost on average every week by each person using the card as a result of card malfunctions and deductions by UK Borders Agency. SYMAAG has also campaigned against the card from before its introduction, and intends to take the matter up with directors of the regional UK Borders Agency.

If you know someone who is forced to live on the cash-less Azure card this Christmas, perhaps they could use their card to pay for £35 of your shopping and you could give them 'cash-back'.
