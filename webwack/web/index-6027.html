Local anti-deportation campaigns update

September 2010

Share on

  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Digg

The Okpara Family are still at risk of deportation, a strong local campaign is continuing to support them, and Sheffield City Council leader Paul Scriven spoke at a recent rally. For more information contact 'Stop the Deportation of the Okpara Family' c/o 07792 605138 or email memesheena@blueyonder.co.uk
Sheffield Star article

The campaign to stop Lemlem Hussein's deportation in July was successful (see Indymedia article ) but the campaign for her right to remain goes on.

The Mtimbusya Family is currently in danger of deportation.

Bavwidi Mpanzu, or 'Djoly' is still in Doncaster prison, sentenced to 18 months for the 'crime' of working to support himself, pay his university course, and help support several Sheffield community groups. He is threatened with deportation at the end of his sentence, and if forcably returned to the Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC) his life will be in danger. The campaign to defend him has very wide support including from David Blunkett MP. Contact Africa Time for more details: 07904 222467 or 07908 321788 or 07961 761899

For more information and petitions for all these people see Sheffield Committee to Defend Asylum Seekers website
