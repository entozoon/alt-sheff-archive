Help save the Al-Dallal family

URGENT: August 2016

Share on

  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Digg

Former university professor Mohammed Al-Dallal was almost killed by militants in his native Iraq after speaking out against corruption. He had five operations, and still suffers various health problems. Later he became cultural attach√© to the Iraqi embassy in Poland, but was subject to further death threats, so he claimed asylum in Britain. His family is settled in Sheffield, their six year old daughter is doing well at school, and he works hard as a volunteer teacher.

But now, after three years rebuilding their lives, they face the trauma of the Home Office trying to deport them to Poland, where they have no friends or family, do not speak the language, and are likely to be targeted both by Iraqi extremists and Polish racists. Under the much-criticised and inflexible "Dublin Regulation", asylum seekers can only claim asylum in their first EU country, regardless of particular circumstances.

A petition for reconsideration of their decision on humanitarian grounds has begun urgently, before a 1st September deadline. It is getting support from many sides including South Yorkshire Migration And Asylum Action Group (SYMAAG) and has been attracting media coverage. Please see the details below and sign the petition.
