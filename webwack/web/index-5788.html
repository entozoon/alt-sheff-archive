Sheffield trees at risk mapped

2015 - 2016

Share on

  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Digg

An online map has been set up to bring together public knowledge about trees on public roads at risk from felling by Amey, under contract with the City Council. The main aim is to record what is happening in the ground and see easily where problems lie, and the scale of the destruction across the city.

Members of the public who see felling notices attached to trees can add to the map. Unfortunately Amey have not published lists of all trees to be felled, possibly because they have not completed their surveys in some areas, but felling notices and attempts to fell have taken place in unlisted areas. The map also shows trees under Tree Preservation Orders (TPOs) which cannot be felled without special permission from the council, but locations of these trees are vague as the published data does not give precise addresses.

The work of compiling the data is going on. If you have any questions or suggestions or want to tell about inaccurate data on the map please contact the website adminstrators.
