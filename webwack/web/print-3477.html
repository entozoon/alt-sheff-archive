Talk: Sex, Race and Class – Jean Rhys and the Brontës

Date, Time & Location Thu 18 Oct 2012, 6.30pm Quaker Meeting House, 10 St James St. S1 2EW
Details Selma James, women's rights and anti-racist campaigner, is an inspiring speaker. In her anthology Sex, Race and Class – the Perspective of Winning: A Selection of Writings 1952-2011 she uncovers sex, race and class as the thorny themes of Jean Rhys and the Brontës. Part of Off the Shelf, Sheffield's 21-year old annual festival of words, with a great programme of over 200 events this year. Tickets £5/£2.50 concessions.
