Heading for Extinction talk

Sat 14 Mar 2020, 2pm, Union St. co-working space, 18-22 Union St. S1 2JP

Find out about Extinction Rebellion, who they are, what it does, why it exists, what it is trying to achieve and how you can join. The planet is in ecological crisis: we are in the midst of the sixth mass extinction event this planet has experienced. Scientists believe we may have entered a period of abrupt climate breakdown. This is an unprecedented global emergency.

In this public talk, members of Extinction Rebellion Sheffield will share the latest climate science on where our planet is heading, discuss some of the current psychology around climate change, and offer solutions through the study of social movements. Everyone is welcome and there will be plenty of time to ask questions and discuss afterwards. Entry is free but please donate towards venue hire costs if you can.

Protest: Shut Down Morton Hall Immigration Detention Centre

Sat 14 March 2020, coach from Sheffield

Join Sheffield and Leeds Student Action for Refugees groups, South Yorkshire Migration and Asylum Action Group (SYMAAG) and These Walls Must Fall supporters on a national, noisy protest at Morton Hall Immigration Detention Centre, Morton Hall Village, Swinderby, Lincolnshire.

Morton Hall is run by the Prison Service for the Home Office and can detain 392 people. Several people have died there in recent years, and detainees have been on hunger strike. The Prisons Inspectors reported on Morton Hall: “Nearly half the detainees told the inspectors they felt depressed or suicidal on arriving”.

Coach transport is being arranged by Sheffield Student Action for Refugees, and SYMAAG. To from Sheffield: Coaches leave from Bar One, Glossop Road (Sheffield University Students Union) at 10am, and then leaving Sheffield Hallam Students Union (near station) at 10.15am. Returning from Morton Hall prompt at 3pm. Book your coach transport from Sheffield at this Eventbrite link.

More info, including flyer to download, and a full report of the previous protests at Morton Hall, on the SYMAAG website.

Solidarity meal in Sheffield after the Morton Hall protest

Sat 14 Mar 2020, 4.30-8pm, Broomhall Community Centre, Broomspring Lane, S10 2FD

Join Mount Pleasant Park Football Club for a solidarity meal following the protest today at Morton Hall Immigration Detention Centre (see above).

An evening of hot food and drinks for all those who went on the demonstration, as well as friends and family and anyone else who is opposed to the UK detention system and hostile environment.

A chance to get warm and dry, and chat to others about migrant solidarity in Sheffield. There will be hot drinks and snacks available from 5pm, and food served from 6pm (small suggested donation for each, or free to asylum seekers and refugees). All food will be vegan. The venue is wheelchair accessible, with an accessible toilet and baby changing facilities on site.
