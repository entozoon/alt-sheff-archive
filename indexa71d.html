Chile: Arpilleras, Photographic and Political Caricatures

From 7pm on Mon 4 to Fri 8 Nov 2013, Exhibition Space, New Jessop Building, University of Sheffield

40 years ago a military coup plunged Chile into a brutal right-wing dictatorship, the first country in the world to experience the shock of "neoliberalism". Many fled the country, some arriving here to make Sheffield their new home.

Now, in commemoration of the anniversary, there is a series of events including this exhibition "Arpilleras, Photographic and Political Caricatures". Launched Monday 4th at 7pm, the exhibition will be on display until 8th November.

Talk: "Fair Admissions to State Schools"

Tue 5 Nov 2013, 8pm The University Arms, Brook Hill, S3 7HG

Mike Granville, Sheffield Humanist Society on the Fair Admissions Campaign. If a state school is designated as "a school with a religious character" it is exempt from the requirement not to discriminate on grounds of religion or belief in relation to admissions even though that school may be funded entirely through general taxation. This means that admissions criteria may give preference to members of a religion. Is this fair?

A talk open to the public, organised by the Sheffield Humanist Society, all welcome.
