Palestine Solidarity Campaign objects to "anti-Semitism" vote

October 2019

Sheffield Palestine Solidarity Campaign (SPSC) have objected to Sheffield City Council adopting the International Holocaust Remembrance Alliance (IHRA) definition of anti-Semitism. They state that although the actual definition is OK, some of its examples of anti-Semitism are not. Believing or saying that Israel is a racist state comes under this. Sadly Israel gives only second-class citizenship (and many other oppressions) to non-Jews, surely an example of racism.

The Council could now, in theory, say that solidarity with the Palestinians is somehow anti-Semitic, and start to cancel venues and speakers.
