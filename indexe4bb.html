Water Voles: Britain's fastest disappearing mammal

Thu 18 Feb 2016, 2pm-3.30pm, Central Library, Surrey St. S1 2LG

An illustrated talk by Christine Gregory, on "Water Voles: Britain's fastest disappearing mammal and their fortunes in Derbyshire". She explores what water voles are and how they live. She considers alien species, climate change and damaged ecosystems as well as the recovery of our rivers and the positive success stories of water vole re-introductions, habitat restoration and conservation.

Free to attend, but places must be booked. Telephone Sheff 2734712 or email information.library@sheffield.gov.uk

Hacking the Body 2.0

An evening featuring two performances: Shutter Flutter and Feel Me, exploring custom-made wearable tech garments. Brought to Access Space by Hacking the Body, a project that explores the current wearable technology fervour, where users‚Äô intimate body data is collected under the pretence of medical or fitness monitoring. The ethics of corporate ownership of body data for consumerist agendas is rarely discussed. The two pieces are the results of questioning how personal body data can demonstrate who we are as individuals. How does accessing our own data enable us to perform our identity?

Film: Priscilla, Queen of the Desert, & Start Your Engines!

Thu 18 Feb 2016, 6pm Showroom Cinema, Paternoster Row S1 2BX

Films and talk to celebrate LGBT History Month. Priscilla took three drag queens in an infamous lavender bus across Australia, turning writer/director Stephan Elliott‚Äôs wild, glittering, flamboyant and fabulously queer road movie into a watershed moment for the LGBT movement. Costumes, cabaret, lip syncing, fights, bigotry and solidarity. The film also highlights the potential of drag to bring together oppressed minorities and imagine new communities that challenge social norms. Clever, funny and beautiful.

The night also features a fascinating talk by Dr. Ben Robbins - a Postdoctoral Fellow at the John F. Kennedy Institute for North American Studies in Berlin. His talk focuses on queer literature, culture, activism and debate from the late nineteenth century to Priscilla, from the early 1990s at the peak of AIDS crisis to the present day.

Start Your Engines! afterwards in the bar, an evening extravaganza of glitter, glamour, disco, sequins and shade. Have you got enough charisma, uniqueness, nerve and talent to take to the runway and lip-sync for your life!?
