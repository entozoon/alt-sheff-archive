11th February 2020

"Migration: Facts and Fictions"

Tue 11 Feb 2014, 1:45-4:45pm, The Workstation, 15 Paternoster Row S1 2BX

A seminar for policy and practice by Sheffield Hallam University.

Speakers:
Emma Briant, University of Sheffield - Migration in the Press and Media
David Robinson, Sheffield Hallam University - Migration and Housing
Hannah Lewis, University of Leeds - Migration and the Labour Market
Jenny Phillimore, University of Birmingham - Migration and Integration
Jo Britton & Majella Kilkey, University of Sheffield - Migration and the Welfare State

This event is free and refreshments will be provided. Places are limited so please contact Emma Smith e.smith@shu.ac.uk or tel. 2253073 to confirm attendance, or with any other queries about the event.

Sheffield Anti-Cuts Alliance meeting

Tue 11 Feb 2014, 7pm Central United Reformed Church, 60 Norfolk St. S1 2JB

SACA has recently been working with the South Yorkshire People's Assembly. At this next SACA meeting, an update on developments with that, and plans for events in 2014.

SACA works to publicise the cuts and support the people of Sheffield to fight back and offer alternatives to these brutal attacks. Get involved! Whether you are an individual or a campaign group who wants to get active, become part of Sheffield Anti-Cuts Alliance. It meets on the 2nd Tuesday of the month. All welcome!

sheffieldanticuts.wordpress.com

"Iterative Cities: Renewal and Revitalisation From The Bottom Up"

Tue 11 Feb 2014, 6pm, Octagon Centre, University of Sheffield, Western Bank S10 2TQ

A public lecture at the University of Sheffield with Marcus Westbury, Founder of Renew Newcastle (Australia) and Renew Australia, on low budget DIY urban renewal schemes which lead to major transformation, as social, cultural and technological factors drive a great surge in creative and economic life at the small scale, in cities and towns around the world.

Entry is free of charge. For more information and to book a place see www.sheffield.ac.uk/whatson/iterativecities

LGBT History Month

Mon 10-Fri 28 Feb 2014, University of Sheffield

Lesbian Gay Bisexual Trans History Month takes place every year in February. It celebrates the lives and achievements of the LGBT community, its diversity and that of society as a whole.

In Sheffield events include talks, films, discussions, documentaries and workshops.

www.lgbthistorymonth.org.uk
