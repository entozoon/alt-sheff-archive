"Debt in Africa: the good, the bad and the ugly"

Date, Time & Location Wed 24 June, 7.15pm, Central United Reformed Church, 60 Norfolk St. S1 2JB
Details Since the financial crisis in 2008, loans to low-income countries have tripled, and now several African countries are heading for dangerous levels of debt once again. Jonathan Stevenson, Campaigns and Communications Officer, Jubilee Debt Campaign, asks why countries across Africa keep on borrowing from foreign lenders. Why do lenders keep on lending? Should debt be avoided altogether? Or is there a 'good' level of debt?
