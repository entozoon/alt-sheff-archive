South Yorkshire Migration and Asylum Action Group (SYMAAG) meeting

Date, Time & Location Tue 5 Apr 2011, 6.30pm Scotia Works, Leadmill Road S1 4SE
Details SYMAAG unites refugee, asylum and migrant organisations with campaign groups and supporting individuals. It campaigns both for the rights of those seeking asylum and of those coming to work in this country. This month: guest speaker from Sheffield Libyan community. The uprising against the Libyan dictatorship has resulted in real hardship for migrant workers trying to leave Libya (mainly to Tunisia); an increase in the number of refugees from Libya and fear for Libyan exiles and students in the UK - one Libyan asylum seeker in Sheffield explained to SYMAAG that he was under threat of deportation to Libya (presumably the UK Borders Agency regards Libya as 'safe'). A speaker from the Libyan community will lead a discussion about the fight against dictatorship; the rights of Libyan refugees and migrant workers in the country; the history of co-operation between European government and Gaddafi in trying to stop immigration from Africa to Europe and what we can do to support the rights of refugees and migrant workers here and in Libya. For more information see: Migrants At Sea: Up to date reports on migration from Libya and its political context De Haas blog on Libya and migration 'Dirty Dealing with Qaddafi' by Frances Webber Human Rights Watch 2009 report on European/Libyan anti-migration co-operation Pictures of migrants at Libyan borders If you want to come to any SYMAAG meeting but find it hard to afford transport don't be put off!
