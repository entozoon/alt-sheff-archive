The Open Rights Group: "Mass Surveillance, Liberty and Activism"

Date, Time & Location Wed 25 Mar 2015, 7.30pm Quaker Meeting House, 10 James St. S1 2EW
Details Jim Killock, executive director of Open Rights Group will give a talk followed by questions from the audience about mass surveillance, liberty and activism. He will say why mass surveillance is a danger to democracy and how we can work together to curb it. More information about Jim Killock here Open Rights Group is a non-profit campaign for digital rights across the UK. They focus on: ineffective internet filters, censorship, copyright and mass surveillance. More info on campaigns at www.openrightsgroup.org/campaigns This event is free and open to the public. You do not need to be an ORG member to attend, just someone interested in an open and free internet. It is helpful but not compulsory to let them know if you will be coming, by email to sheffield@openrightsgroup.org or via the website below. Arrive from 7.15pm onwards for a 7.30pm start.
