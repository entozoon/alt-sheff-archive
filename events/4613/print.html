Film: The Royal Road

Date, Time & Location Mon 13–Sat 18 Jun 2016, 2pm & 4pm daily, Bloc Projects, 71 Eyre Lane, S1 4RB
Details A cinematic essay in defense of remembering, exploring the Spanish colonization of California and the Mexican-American War alongside intimate reflections on nostalgia, butch identity and Alfred Hitchcock’s Vertigo. Omitting the depiction of a single human body, the film combines rigorous historical research with a lyrical stream-of-consciousness monologue, set against landscapes of urban California. Part of Sheffield Fringe 2016: Object Documentary. The biennial Sheffield Fringe 2016 is held at Bloc Projects, in Sheffield’s Cultural Industries Quarter, coinciding with Sheffield International Documentary Film Festival (Doc/Fest). A nine-day programme of film screenings, talks and performances. Many of the short documentaries and artists’ films have very interesting politically and socially engaged content. All events are free and booking is not required. Bloc Projects
