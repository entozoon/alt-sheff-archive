SHARROW FESTIVAL - resheduled!

Sat 22 Aug 2015, till 8pm, Mount Pleasant Park, Sharrow Lane, nr London Rd. S11 8AE

Sharrow's free multicultural festival was postponed due to rain earlier this year, so it's back on again in August.

Assuming the previous plans hold, expect a special covered Community Stage, and a hip-hop jam in the all-weather pitch with Chillfoo Front (sounds) and Marco (paint jam). Earth First Summer Gathering

19 - 24 Aug 2015, The Peak District

Hate Fracking? Join Earth First for an amazing week of skill-sharing, networking and fun aimed at making the anti-fracking movement (and others) as strong, dynamic and strategic as possible. Earth First! is based on principles of non-hierarchical organisation and direct action to stop the destruction of the Earth and its inhabitants. Full details on their website.

South Yorkshire is under licence to be fracked. If this goes ahead our water, soil and air is likely to be poisoned beyond repair, by this dangerous new experimental technique. The only answer is to get together and stop it from happening before it is too late.

DONCASTER PRIDE 2015

Sat 22 Aug 2015, 1pm-9pm Sir Nigel Gresley Square, Waterdale, Doncaster (town centre)

The 9th annual Doncaster Pride festival celebrating lesbian, gay bisexual and transgender (LGBT) culture is open to all. In fact the organisers are positively promoting the event to anyone in Doncaster and surrounding areas as a way to bring together the wider community to enjoy the day with them. They have also teamed up this year with local branch NUM and local collieries, including the recently closed Hatfield Colliery, to celebrate the 30th anniversary of the miners returning to work after the strike. An unusual combination of groups? Well, not really, as anyone who saw the film "Pride" will know. There is history between the mining communities and the LGBT community dating back to the 80s when the group Lesbian and Gays support the Mineworkers (LGSM) raised money for striking miners and their families. There will be miners carrying banners, Women Against Pit Closures and possibly even the LGSM members from London. Sheffield City Pipe Band will lead the march, giving the mining communities of Doncaster a reason to march, unfurl their banners proudly and lead the walk through the streets of Doncaster.

The main stage features over 8 hours of dance, music and drag as well as market stalls, fairground rides and information providers including Doncaster Pride Youth Project that is working in Doncaster schools to help support young people, families and educators to stamp out homophobia in schools and deal with issues around sexuality.
