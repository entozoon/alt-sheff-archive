Public meeting: Sheffield Voices for the Five Campaign

Tue 25 Feb 2014, 7.30pm, United Reform Church 60 Norfolk St. S1 2 JB

Sheffield Voices for the Five Campaign is the local voice of the international Voices for the Five Campaign to secure freedom for the Cuban anti-terrorism agents who remain unjustly imprisoned after 15 years in US jails. This public meeting is part of the lead up to the international Commission reviewing the case. Speakers include Geoff Bottoms and Steve Cottingham.

Note the venue is opposite the stage doors of the Crucible theatre.

LGBT History Month

Mon 10-Fri 28 Feb 2014, University of Sheffield

Lesbian Gay Bisexual Trans History Month takes place every year in February. It celebrates the lives and achievements of the LGBT community, its diversity and that of society as a whole.

Film: "Roadmap to Apartheid"

Tue 25 Feb 2014, 7.30pm, Sheffield University, Students Union Auditorium

A free showing of the multiple award winning film "Roadmap to Apartheid" as part of Israeli Apartheid Week 2014. Narrated by Alice Walker (author of The Color Purple) it covers the rise and fall of apartheid in South Africa, and why many Palestinians feel they are living in an apartheid system today, and why an increasing number of people around the world agree with them. The film documents the life of Palestinians in the West Bank, Gaza Strip and inside Israel, and includes contributions by respected analysts on the subject.
