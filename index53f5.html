LIBFEST

Sat 29 Aug 2015, 11am‚Äì10pm Old Junior School, South View Rd. (off Sharrow Lane) S7 1DB

Come and enjoy LibFest, a grassroots community music festival. A variety of live music and DJs, food and drink throughout the day. Stalls and workshops run by various groups.

LibFest presents a positive alternative to austerity policies following the general election result and in response to the worsening economic conditions for working people. A day of solidarity and hope, connecting diverse communities concerned about the direction our society is taking. It will be an opportunity for the people of Sheffield to meet like-minded folk, exchange ideas and celebrate progressive models for running our communities and workplaces, and the fact that there are masses of people across our city working for positive changes to our lives.

If your organisation would like to help by publicising, attending, or making the event as good as possible through running a stall and/or workshop or anything else, contact the organisers. Printed and online publicity is available to distribute.

"Vintage by the Kilo" pop-up clothes sale

Fri 28-Sat 29 Aug 2015, 11am-5pm, Theatre Delicatessen, The Moor (old Woolworths shop) S1 3LR

Yorkshire legends "Vintage by the Kilo" are changing the face of fashion, one kilogramme at a time. Their 'weigh-and-pay' mega-collection outsizes any charity shop, and all carefully chosen stuff, not just 'jumble'. No disrespect to charity or jumble sales, but this is mainly quality 60s, 70s, 80s and 90s clothing which would have been pricy first time round. The openings have been popping up since 1997, and this year they're at Theatre Delicatessen and other venues about twice a month. It's surely only a matter of time before they get a thrift shop of their own. Get down for a look before they're famous.

Every second-hand purchase you make is a nail in the coffin for the fashion industry which makes corporate shareholders obscenely rich by paying exploitation wages, highly polluting from manufacture to transportation, and leading vulnerable people into expensive, illusory lives as fashion-victims. Don't believe the hype; get yer grunge gear here.
