Morton Hall Detention Centre protest details

October 2019

Share on

  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Digg

It’s just days until the biggest protest so far planned against Morton Hall Detention Centre near Lincoln. The protest is organised by SYMAAG (South Yorkshire Migration and Asylum Action Group) and STAR (Student Action for Refugees). This is an update from SYMAAG about transport to Morton Hall, and what to expect on the day.

Almost 30,000 people are being taken from our communities and locked up in prison-like detention centres, without time limit, with no idea of when they might be released. Why? They just don’t have the correct immigration papers. This is unacceptable. This has to end. For more details see the website of These Walls Must Fall (www.detention.org.uk).

On Saturday 26th October there will be a demonstration against the cruel and inhumane treatment of detainees at Morton Hall Detention Centre, in Lincolnshire. It is a prison contracted to the Home Office as an Immigration Removal Centre (IRC) as part of the UK governments "hostile environment" policy. It has room to detain 392 people, and there have been several deaths in recent years inside its walls, with nearly half the detainees feeling suicidal or depressed according to a 2017 Prison Inspectors report.

Join SYMAAG, STAR, These Walls Must Fall, and many others at the demonstration. The actual protest is 12-3pm, with banners and chanting while surrounding Morton Hall (Morton Hall Village, Swinderby, Lincoln LN6 9PT).

People detained at Morton Hall welcome protests, and are encouraged by them. This message was sent to SYMAAG after the last protest there, from some of the people who are detained inside: “Yesterday was full of joy and atonement as we had protesters showing their solidarity for us. It was reviving to know other people are concerned with our despicable situation and support us. Thank you for braving obstacles to come to support us"

For those who want to come to the protest but might be feeling worried about it, Student Action for Refugees have written this great document about keeping safe and how we will be supporting each other on the day at Morton Hall.

Transport: in the wider region, groups like Leeds Uni Oxfam Society, and Manchester Momentum have organised transport to Morton Hall, and everyone is welcome to join them (details below), or make your own way there. If you are going, now’s the time to make sure your transport is arranged. The full list of travel from cities organised so far is below, and if you don’t see something in your area listed, please make arrangements to come with people in your area, by coach, train, or carpool.

Tickets for Coach Travel from Sheffield, click here. Tickets are £10 if you can afford it. If not, pay what you can or reserve a free ticket. Transport from Sheffield leaves at 10am, returning around 5pm to Sheffield.

Transport from Manchester: Please contact 07956 805974 (text or call) to book a place. Free places for asylum seekers/unwaged migrants.

Transport from Leeds: please contact laurasargen@live.co.uk or call 07377 400788, to book a place.

Transport from Nottingham & Lincoln: Trains go to Swinderby station, which is a 15 minute walk from Morton Hall. If you would like to find someone to get the train with, you might find it useful to post in the Facebook event above.

Transport from London: there may be a coach organised, but we do not have the details yet. Please keep an eye on the Facebook event for updates, and if you organise transport that we can advertise, get in touch to let us know the details.

The protest is part of a weekend of action in Sheffield about immigration detention:

Saturday 26th October, evening social at the Broomhall Centre after the Morton Hall demo, with discussion about resistance to the border regime. It includes screening of a new film made by Mount Pleasant Park Football Club, Victor Mujakachi from ASSIST Sheffield, and These Walls Must Fall.

Sunday 27th October, 5-a-side football tournament at the U Mix Centre, 10.30am-3pm. Run by Mount Pleasant Park Football Team, with international teams, the event is a fundraiser for SYMAAG. Come to watch or play!
