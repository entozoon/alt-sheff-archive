New Mini Orchards Project

24th September 2012 deadline

Share on

  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Digg

The Abundance project, Grow Sheffield and tree-planting charity Trees for Cities.are extremely excited to announce The Mini Orchards Project.

They are looking for local groups, schools, community groups, tenant and resident associations and voluntary projects to plant orchards and mini orchards across the city over the next 9 months.

If your group can answer yes to the following questions and is keen to set up a fruit tree project please fill in a form registering your interest and tell a little more about your ideas.

Are you interested in creating a community orchard on your site?
Do you have a space of at least 75m2 available for planting (enough for at least 5 trees)?
Do you have landowner permission to plant trees on the land or can you get this in writing?
Do you have a group of willing volunteers to undertake the planting activities?
Do you have a group of willing volunteers to undertake necessary aftercare of the trees?
Can you produce a 5 year care plan for the trees showing how they will be maintained and incorporated into your group activities?

All applications will be reviewed by a panel and successful groups will be informed by the first week of October 2012. Planting and other activities will take place between October 2012 and March 2013.

To request an expression of interest form or for more information please email Coralie on coralie.grow@gmail.com

The closing date for receiving forms is Monday 24th September, 2012 at 5pm.
