'Bedroom Tax' protest growing

Summer 2013

Share on

  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Digg

Sheffield City Council is starting to take tenants to court because they haven't paid their Council Tax. This is only the beginning of the nightmare for people dependent on benefits that have been cut. Over five thousand Sheffield households are losing 14%-28% of their weekly benefits due to the new 'Bedroom Tax' (under-occupation penalty) while Sheffield has more households waiting for a 1-bedroom council house than there are council houses of every size in the city. Up to 23% of council tax is also being introduced for many people who previously were not liable.

Councils across the country have taken action to protect local tenants from the government's action by either refusing to evict tenants or 'reclassifying' bedrooms as other types of room. Sheffield City Council has so far refused to join in any of these things to protect some of the city's most vulnerable people.

Whether or not this affects you, please sign this online petition trying to get at least 5,000 signatures.
