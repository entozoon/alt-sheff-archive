University of Sheffield occupied

March 2016

Share on

  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Digg

UPDATE: Action suspended on 9th March; a full statement online at the link below ends "This is not the end of the Free University of Sheffield in action, it is the beginning of the Free University as an idea and as a movement."

The University of Sheffield’s Richard Roberts Auditorium was occupied from 29th February. In a detailed statement, The Free University of Sheffield group says "the struggle is against this government and the neoliberal society it’s situated within, which values profit over people, productivity over joy; war over education". They object to university marketisation, where students are treated as consumers; passive recipients of information for the sake of employability rather than producers of knowledge. Academics should likewise have autonomy to encourage an environment of exchange, not limited by measures like the Teaching Excellence Framework

They are not criticising management or individual academics, and will not be disrupting learning; during lectures they are sitting to the side. Instead, and importantly, they are trying to realise an alternative model of democratic learning. In free periods in the lecture theatre they are creating an open zone of exchange, working for education that is liberating and transformative of self and society. "Buzzwords such as ‘open university’ and ‘widening participation’ are thrown around until they become empty concepts, used to entrench and legitimate their exact opposite" they say.

The full statement is available on The Free University of Sheffield Facebook page. Supporters are warmly invited to join them.
