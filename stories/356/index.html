Good News: Sheffield to put cycling first

March 2014

Share on

  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Digg

Sheffield council are keen to promote cycling for reasons of health, air quality, road safety, congestion, etc. While they have made some progress, it seems that they've realised there 's a lot more they could do. An instruction from John Bann, Head of Transport, Traffic and Parking Services signals a shift of emphasis. Two main changes will be made; The first is for planners to "think cycling" from the outset, before it is too late to redesign, and lesser quality cycling facilities have to be "bolted on" to plans at the end. The second is a "Cycle Audit" to be integrated into all project planning and design work for schemes to the public highway and public realm. It will apply to all roads and anything that impacts public highways, like housing, retail and industrial planning.

The move follows much work by campaigners, especially CycleSheffield, who welcomed this as the start to some joined up thinking - but will be checking that this advice and its sentiment is put into practice. CycleSheffield is an active and effective group. Why not join them if you are a Sheffield cyclist? They welcome newcomers, and annual membership is very inexpensive at £4/£6.

Full story at CycleSheffield website
