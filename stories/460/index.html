Gay Forces apology demanded

Petition deadline 17th August 2016

Share on

  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Digg

Sheffielder Jacqui De La Maziere has started a petition asking the UK Government to apologise to all ex-Forces personnel who were dismissed for being gay. HM Forces men and women were investigated and discharged from the Armed forces, simply for being gay, over many years. They suffered emotional, mental and material scars that still last today - homelessness, loss of friends, job and pension, rejection by family, and still no alternative career. Apparently in 2007, an apology was issued by the MOD but this does not count as an apology to the individuals.

In a letter to the Guardian by Jacqui, 7 years ago, she wrote of people not necessarily knowing they were gay when joining the Forces as teenagers, and being made homeless and forced to come out to their families.

The Queen and the Government have so far refused to get involved. The matter was taken up in Parliament in 2015 by Paul Blomfield, MP for Sheffield Central. Please help to keep the pressure up.

The petition needs 10,000 signatures, and must be signed by 17th August 2016

https://petition.parliament.uk/petitions/121652
