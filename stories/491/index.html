Sheffield visit to asylum seeker prison

December 2016

Share on

  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Digg

On Saturday 3rd December 2016, two coaches left Sheffield to join a 2000 strong demonstration at Yarls Wood Detention Centre, run by the multinational corporation Serco for the UK government. It which houses female asylum seekers, and featured in a 2015 Channel 4 documentary exposing abuse of detainees.

Many of the women have lived in the UK for years and have partners and children here. They are often removed in the middle of the night, locked up, or put on charter flights without notice. Many are survivors of sexual abuse, some are pregnant, incidents of self-harm and attempted suicide are high.

Visiting like this is a powerful experience, demonstrators use loudspeakers to communicate directly with the women inside, and hear their stories. Serco moved the women to another part of the prison as the protest gathered outside this time, to make it more difficult for them to see and hear, so the demonstrators concentrated on making more noise, kicking the perimeter fence and shouting.

The demonstration was organised by Movement for Justice and attended by people from many different campaigns including Sheffield Student Action for Refugees (Facebook page here).

The campaign is building momentum, getting bigger all the time, and more demonstrations are planned. For more information and to get involved, or just to keep up to date with future events see the website below.
