Kelham Island to get hydro-powered?

December 2010

Share on

  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Digg

Sheffield Renewables has applied for planning permission to build a hydropower scheme on Kelham Island in Sheffield, in collaboration with City Council Regeneration Officers, and Kelham Island Museum.

Generating around 75MWh of electricity per year (20 typical homes) it will be a modern waterwheel in an existing wheel pit. It's one of the earliest industrial sites in Sheffield, possibly used for water power in the Middle Ages. The wheel will provide an educational visitor attraction.

Sheffield Renewables is a not-for-profit community and social enterprise that develops renewable energy schemes run by and for local people. Surplus is re-invested to support local sustainability. They welcome comments and questions, especially from anyone living nearby. They also offer a range of volunteering activities to suit different interests, experience and time you can spare.
