"Trashing Transphobia" free film-making course

August until November 2015

Share on

  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Digg

A new course run by E.D.E.N will produce a film that tackles the widespread issues of discrimination, bullying and prejudice that many transgender people face in the UK today. Expanding on the success of previous film-making projects educating society on topics relating to the lesbian, gay, bisexual and transgender community, the project will run weekday evenings from August until November. It welcomes anyone who self-identifies as part of the LGBTQIA+ community. With the help of industry experts it will guide participants through workshops and one-on-one support.

The workshops will train participants to use professional DSLR video equipment and to record sound as well as develop narration, production and editing skills. They will also include training on transphobia. These skills will then be utilised to develop, produce, record and edit an educational feature length documentary to be screened on Transgender Day of Remembrance, 20 November 2015. The project is funded by the Big Lottery Fund. To book a place email info@edenfilm.co.uk or phone 07725 911736.
