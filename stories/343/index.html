WDM in food speculation campaign victory

January 2014 news

Share on

  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Digg

After campaigning by the WDM (World Development Movement) and other pressure groups, EU negotiators agreed in January to introduce regulation to prevent the worst excesses of speculation by banks and hedge funds driving up food prices and worsening the global hunger crisis. Once officially approved by MEPs and EU finance ministers, it must be incorporated into national legislation in each of the 28 EU member states. WDM has hailed the decision as an historic step forward, but said that the UK government's opposition to tough controls has resulted in serious loopholes in the regulation. They are now urging the European regulator ESMA to ensure that the new rules are implemented effectively, and not watered down further by industry lobbying.

Speculation increases price volatility and has been a major factor in the sharp spikes in global food prices of the last six years.
