Universal Jobmatch and the surveillance of jobseekers

Wed 16 Apr 2014, 7pm University Arms, 197 Brook Hill S3 7HG

(NOTE: THIS EVENT WAS PREVIOUSLY PLANNED FOR 9th APRIL)

Job-seekers claiming Job Seeker's Allowance are required to use the Government's online recruitment search site 'Universal Jobmatch'. This is more than just a website; it's a tool that Job Centre advisors can use to monitor job hunting and enforce sanctions. It monitors job searches made, time spent on the site, jobs applied for and those turned down. There are data insecurities associated with the site, enabling a situation where 50% of ads are fraudulent, and bogus employers are accessing private data. Unlike other recruitment services or search engines, job seekers don't always have a choice to pick a better service.

The meeting will be discussing more about what Universal Jobmatch is and how this system of surveillance is having a real effect on people's lives.
