Global Justice Sheffield meeting

Thu 3 Mar 2016, 7pm, Dog & Partridge, 56 Trippet Lane S1 4EL

Monthly meeting of Global Justice Sheffield, to discuss and plan upcoming events and campaigns including discussing contributions to the 16th April city centre action.

Global Justice Now (formerly World Development Movement) aims to change the actions, policies and institutions of governments, corporations and wealthy minorities, which are preventing an end to poverty and injustice. Part of a global movement, it works alongside people protesting in the developing world. Meetings are usually followed by informal gathering in a nearby pub.

Interested in volunteering?

Thu 3 Mar 2016, 4pm-6pm, ASSIST Office, Victoria Hall S1 2JB

Refused asylum seekers are not allowed to work, to register themselves as homeless or to be placed in local authority night shelters. Many become destitute, struggling daily for food, accommodation and clothing. ASSIST works with asylum seekers in this situation. If you are interested in volunteering, come to one of their "Drop in and check it out" sessions. Meet current volunteers, find out more about the different volunteering opportunities, chat about what might suit you and apply to be a volunteer with ASSIST.

The ASSIST office, Victoria Hall has its entrance on Chapel Walk, opposite the back of Marks and Spencer. Visitors can arrive and leave at any time during each session, and there is a short presentation at 10 minutes past each hour. To find out more, contact Sheff 2754960 or email volunteer@assistsheffield.org.uk

Debate: Is Fairtrade Enough?

Thu 3 Mar 2016, 6-8pm HUBS Building, Paternoster Row S1 2QQ

No one would argue that Fairtrade is a bad thing, but does it go far enough? In recent years several studies have cast doubt on the impact Fairtrade accreditation has on the developing world. An informal discussion with speakers about the impact of Fairtrade and what consumers can do to ensure that their choices have a positive impact.

Organised by Sheffield Hallam Students Union and Sheffield Festival of Debate, hosted by Opus Independents which also publishes the "Localcheck" Alt-Sheff column in "Now Then" magazine. Free to attend, but please register so they can get an idea of numbers.
