UBI Lab Meet-up

Wed 3 Jul 2019, 6.30pm, Quaker Meeting House, St. James St. S1 2EW

Sheffield City Council has passed a motion giving its support to a pilot of Universal Basic Income (UBI) in the city. Sheffield could become the first English city to trial universal basic income. Join this UBI Lab Meet-up with a speaker for the first hour, and Working Group Update for the second hour.

Jason Leman, Chair of UBI Lab Sheffield, says "Sheffield has a long history of leading radical change, from the Chartists through to the Socialist Republic of South Yorkshire. It helps people who are looking after their family and it could also give people the confidence to retrain, volunteer or become entrepreneurs."

Petition for the Council to recognise State of Palestine

Wed 3 Jul 2019, from 1pm, Sheffield Town Hall, Pinstone St. S1 2HH

A petition with over 10,000 signatures calls on the Council to recognise State of Palestine. It will be presented to Julie Dore, Leader of Sheffield City Council, in line with a cross-party motion passed in the UK Parliament in 2014 and UN official designation since 1988.

The size of the petition is unprecedented and demonstrates the wish of ordinary citizens to see justice for Palestinians, who have suffered dispossession of their homes, Israeli military occupation, human rights abuse and siege over the decades since 1948.

Whilst largely symbolic, Sheffieldâ€™s recognition of the Palestinian right to statehood would be significant. The UK government has always taken a timid approach under United States pressure, but many feel that the tide of public opinion is turning. Sheffield City Council would be the first UK council to take this step and set a precedent, just as happened with its recognition of Somaliland as an independent state, which other councils followed. Sheffield has a strong tradition of supporting international campaigns for justice, such as the anti-apartheid movement.

Before the meeting of full Council, speakers will address a rally at 1pm outside the Town Hall, and the Palestinian flag will be raised. Councillor Julie Dore will be invited to receive the petition.
