"Principle Five" Co-operative Resource Centre launch

Wed 19 Feb 2014, 12 noon, Aizlewoods Mill, Nursery Street S3 8GG

Yorkshire Co-operative Resource Centre public launch, as a resource base and information centre about the co-operative movement set up by Co-operatives Yorkshire and Humber.

Rachael Vorberg-Rugh, co-author of Building Co-operation will give a talk and sign copies of the book which will be on sale.

Linda Gomila from the Co-operative Group will be available to answer questions on Widening Opportunities for Women (WOW)

"Co-operatives provide education and training for their members, elected representatives, managers, and employees so they can contribute effectively to the development of their co-operatives. They inform the general public - particularly young people and opinion leaders - about the nature and benefits of co-operation". Principle 5 of the international co-operative principles

"Principle Five" (Yorkshire Co-operative Resource Centre) is in the process of becoming a multi-stakeholder co-operative for the purpose of co-operative education, as an information hub, library and archive for the co-operative movement in the region, and a friendly and accessible place where anyone can learn more about the movement. It is based at Aizlewood's Mill, Sheffield, a co-operatively owned building and home of the Sheffield Co-operative Development Group. Contact for any details steve.thompson@phonecoop.coop

Entry to this launch event is free, but please book your place through: Eventbrite booking. Refreshments provided.

"The Quiet Deportation" Palestinian residency rights and colonisation in Jerusalem

Wed 19 Feb 2014, 7.15pm Friends Meeting House, St James St. S1 2EW

Since 1967, more than 14,000 Jerusalemites have lost their residency IDs and, as such, their right to live in their city of birth. Termed the "quiet deportation", Israel's creeping annexation is organised through a web of discriminatory policies - including ID revocation, the ban on family unification, and barriers to child registration. This presentation by Megan Driscoll will delve into these measures, examine their far-reaching consequences, and focus on what steps can be taken to pressure Israel to end its efforts at ethnic cleansing.

A public meeting run by Sheffield Palestinian Solidarity Campaign.
